# CCPA Compliance Checklist

## Overview

The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) is California's comprehensive privacy law that went into effect on January 1, 2020. It was amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), with most provisions taking effect on January 1, 2023, and enforcement by the California Privacy Protection Agency beginning July 1, 2023.

**Applies to**: Businesses that:
- Have gross annual revenues exceeding $25 million; OR
- Buy, sell, or share personal information of 100,000 or more California consumers or households annually; OR  
- Derive 50% or more of annual revenues from selling or sharing consumers' personal information

**AND** do business in California or collect personal information from California residents.

**Key Concepts**:
- **Consumer**: California resident
- **Personal Information**: Information that identifies, relates to, or could be linked to a particular consumer or household
- **Business**: For-profit entity doing business in California that meets thresholds
- **Sell**: Making personal information available to third parties for monetary or other valuable consideration
- **Share**: Making personal information available to third parties for cross-context behavioral advertising

## Core Requirements

### 1. Notice at Collection

**Requirement**: Provide notice at or before collecting personal information.

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Categories of personal information to be collected
- [ ] Purposes for which categories will be used
- [ ] Whether information is sold or shared
- [ ] Length of time information will be retained (or criteria for determining retention)
- [ ] Link to privacy policy

**Timing**:
- [ ] At or before point of collection
- [ ] Update if new category or purpose added

**Best Practice**: Use concise, clear language; avoid legalese

### 2. Privacy Policy

**Requirement**: Maintain and make available a comprehensive privacy policy.

**Must Include**:
- [ ] Categories of personal information collected (last 12 months)
- [ ] Categories of sources from which information is collected
- [ ] Business or commercial purposes for collecting information
- [ ] Categories of third parties with whom information is shared
- [ ] Categories of personal information sold or shared (last 12 months)
  - For each category: categories of third parties to whom sold/shared
- [ ] Categories of personal information disclosed for business purpose (last 12 months)
  - For each category: categories of third parties to whom disclosed
- [ ] Categories of sensitive personal information collected and purposes
- [ ] Length of time business intends to retain each category (or criteria)
- [ ] Consumer rights and how to exercise them
- [ ] Instructions for submitting requests
- [ ] Contact information for privacy questions

**Accessibility**:
- [ ] Prominently displayed on website homepage
- [ ] Conspicuous "Privacy Policy" or "Privacy Notice" link
- [ ] Updated at least once every 12 months

### 3. Right to Know

**Requirement**: Enable consumers to request information about data collected.

**Consumers Can Request**:
- [ ] Categories of personal information collected
- [ ] Categories of sources
- [ ] Business or commercial purpose for collecting or selling
- [ ] Categories of third parties to whom information was disclosed or sold
- [ ] Specific pieces of personal information collected

**Response Requirements**:
- [ ] Respond within **45 days** (45-day extension allowed with notice)
- [ ] Provide information for preceding **12 months**
- [ ] Deliver electronically in portable, readily useable format
- [ ] Free of charge (up to 2 requests per 12-month period)
- [ ] May charge reasonable fee for excessive or repetitive requests

**What to Provide**:
- [ ] Two or more methods to submit requests (toll-free number, website, email, etc.)
- [ ] Process to verify consumer identity
- [ ] No requirement to create account to submit request

### 4. Right to Delete

**Requirement**: Enable consumers to request deletion of personal information.

**Obligations**:
- [ ] Delete consumer's personal information from records
- [ ] Direct service providers to delete
- [ ] Respond within **45 days** (45-day extension allowed)
- [ ] Confirm deletion or explain why deletion was not required

**Exceptions** (May Retain When Necessary To):
- Complete transaction or provide requested goods/services
- Detect security incidents, protect against fraud, or debug
- Exercise free speech, ensure another's free speech, or exercise legal rights
- Comply with California Electronic Communications Privacy Act
- Engage in scientific, historical, or statistical research in public interest
- Enable solely internal uses reasonably aligned with consumer expectations
- Comply with legal obligation
- Make other internal and lawful uses compatible with context of provision

**Process**:
- [ ] Provide two or more methods to submit deletion requests
- [ ] Verify consumer identity before deleting
- [ ] Separately confirm deletion before processing (optional but recommended)

### 5. Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing

**Requirement**: Enable consumers to opt out of sale or sharing of personal information.

**"Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" Link**:
- [ ] Conspicuous link on homepage
- [ ] Link titled exactly "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" (or "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" if not sharing)
- [ ] Opt-out mechanism that doesn't require account creation
- [ ] Process opt-out within **15 business days**
- [ ] Wait at least **12 months** before asking to opt back in

**Additional Requirements**:
- [ ] Respect Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals as valid opt-out
- [ ] Notify all third parties to whom information was sold/shared
- [ ] Do not sell/share information after opt-out
- [ ] Do not discriminate against consumers who opt out

**For Children**:
- [ ] Under 13: Require opt-in consent from parent/guardian
- [ ] Ages 13-15: Require opt-in consent from child
- [ ] Age 16+: Opt-out applies

### 6. Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information

**Requirement**: Enable consumers to limit use of sensitive personal information.

**Sensitive Personal Information Includes**:
- Social security, driver's license, state ID, passport number
- Account login credentials
- Financial account information
- Precise geolocation
- Racial or ethnic origin, religious or philosophical beliefs, union membership
- Contents of mail, email, text messages (if not directed to business)
- Genetic data
- Biometric information for identification
- Health information
- Sex life or sexual orientation

**"Limit the Use of My Sensitive Personal Information" Link**:
- [ ] Required only if using sensitive PI for purposes beyond:
  - Providing requested goods/services
  - Security and integrity
  - Short-term, transient use
  - Performing services on behalf of business
  - Quality and safety verification
  - Specified permissible purposes in regulations
- [ ] Conspicuous link on homepage (if required)
- [ ] Process request within **15 business days**
- [ ] Notify third parties to limit use

### 7. Right to Correct

**Requirement**: Enable consumers to request correction of inaccurate personal information.

**Obligations**:
- [ ] Use commercially reasonable efforts to correct information
- [ ] Respond within **45 days** (45-day extension allowed)
- [ ] Take into account nature of personal information and purposes
- [ ] Notify service providers and contractors to correct

**Process**:
- [ ] Provide method to submit correction requests
- [ ] Verify consumer identity
- [ ] Inform service providers and contractors of correction

### 8. Right to Non-Discrimination

**Requirement**: Do not discriminate against consumers who exercise their rights.

**Prohibited Actions**:
- [ ] Denying goods or services
- [ ] Charging different prices or rates
- [ ] Providing different level or quality of goods or services
- [ ] Suggesting consumer will receive different price, rate, level, or quality

**Financial Incentive Programs Allowed** (With Conditions):
- [ ] Provide notice of financial incentive program terms
- [ ] Obtain opt-in consent
- [ ] Explain how incentive is reasonably related to value of consumer's data
- [ ] Allow opt-out at any time
- [ ] Do not use unjust, unreasonable, coercive, or usurious methods

### 9. Verification of Consumer Requests

**Requirement**: Implement reasonable verification procedures.

**Standard**:
- [ ] Verify identity to **reasonable degree of certainty**
- [ ] Match data points provided in request to data maintained
- [ ] Use more stringent verification for sensitive information or deletion

**Methods**:
- [ ] Two or three pieces of personal information already collected
- [ ] Declaration under penalty of perjury
- [ ] May use third-party identity verification services
- [ ] Cannot require account creation solely for verification

**Authorized Agent**:
- [ ] Allow consumers to use authorized agents
- [ ] Require written permission from consumer
- [ ] Verify agent's authority
- [ ] May require consumer to verify identity directly with business
- [ ] May deny request if agent does not submit proof

### 10. Contractual Requirements for Service Providers

**Requirement**: Enter into contracts with service providers.

**Service Provider Contract Must**:
- [ ] Prohibit service provider from selling or sharing personal information
- [ ] Prohibit retaining, using, or disclosing PI for any purpose other than performing services
- [ ] Prohibit combining PI with information from other sources (with exceptions)
- [ ] Allow consumers to exercise rights with respect to PI
- [ ] Grant business right to take reasonable steps to ensure service provider uses PI consistent with obligations
- [ ] Require service provider to notify business if it determines it can no longer meet obligations

**Contractors and Third Parties**:
- [ ] Similar requirements apply to contractors (slightly different obligations than service providers)
- [ ] Third parties (who are not service providers/contractors) may use PI for their own purposes

### 11. Data Minimization

**Requirement**: Collect, use, retain, and share only personal information reasonably necessary and proportionate to achieve purposes.

**Requirements**:
- [ ] Collect only personal information necessary for disclosed purposes
- [ ] Retain personal information only as long as reasonably necessary
- [ ] Do not collect additional categories without providing new notice
- [ ] Do not use for purposes incompatible with disclosed purposes

**CPRA Addition** (Effective January 1, 2023):
- [ ] Limit collection to what is reasonably necessary and proportionate
- [ ] Avoid collecting information when purpose can be achieved with less intrusive means

### 12. Sensitive Personal Information Limitations

**Requirement**: Provide heightened protection for sensitive personal information.

**Requirements**:
- [ ] Disclose collection and use of sensitive personal information in privacy policy
- [ ] Obtain consent for use beyond permissible purposes (or offer opt-out)
- [ ] Do not use or disclose for purposes other than those specified in regulations without opt-in consent
- [ ] Allow consumers to limit use and disclosure

**Permissible Uses Without Opt-In**:
- Providing goods/services requested
- Security and integrity
- Short-term, transient use
- Performing services for business
- Quality verification and improvement
- Specific purposes in regulations

### 13. Recordkeeping

**Requirement**: Maintain records to demonstrate compliance.

**Must Maintain**:
- [ ] Metrics on consumer requests:
  - Number of requests received
  - Number complied with (in whole or in part)
  - Number denied
  - Median number of days to respond
- [ ] Record requests for at least 24 months
- [ ] Disclose metrics in privacy policy (updated annually)

**Additional Records**:
- [ ] Privacy policy versions
- [ ] Data inventory (categories, sources, purposes, recipients)
- [ ] Vendor contracts and due diligence
- [ ] Training records
- [ ] Data retention policies
- [ ] Security measures documentation

### 14. Training

**Requirement**: Train employees who handle consumer inquiries.

**Training Should Cover**:
- [ ] CCPA requirements and consumer rights
- [ ] How to direct consumers to exercise rights
- [ ] How to process and respond to requests
- [ ] Verification procedures
- [ ] Record-keeping requirements
- [ ] Non-discrimination requirements

**Best Practices**:
- [ ] Provide training to all customer-facing staff
- [ ] Update training annually or when regulations change
- [ ] Document training completion
- [ ] Include privacy training in onboarding

### 15. Risk Assessment

**Requirement** (CPRA): Conduct regular cybersecurity audits.

**Requirements**:
- [ ] Assess risks to security of personal information
- [ ] Implement reasonable security procedures and practices
- [ ] Conduct regular and ongoing security audits
- [ ] Address identified risks

**Reasonable Security Includes**:
- [ ] Encryption of personal information
- [ ] Access controls
- [ ] Monitoring and detection
- [ ] Incident response procedures
- [ ] Vendor security requirements

## Penalties

**Civil Penalties**:
- **Up to $2,500 per violation**
- **Up to $7,500 per intentional violation**
- **30-day cure period** for violations (business must be given opportunity to cure)

**Private Right of Action** (Data Breaches):
- **$100-$750 per consumer per incident** (or actual damages, whichever is greater)
- Available for unauthorized access and theft of specified personal information
- No cure period for data breaches
- Class action lawsuits allowed

**California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA)**:
- New enforcement agency created by CPRA
- Formally established July 1, 2021; enforcement authority began July 1, 2023
- Has rulemaking and enforcement authority

## Quick Checklist

**Website Updates**:
- [ ] Privacy policy updated and accessible from homepage
- [ ] "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link (if selling/sharing)
- [ ] "Limit the Use of My Sensitive Personal Information" link (if required)
- [ ] Notice at collection on data collection pages

**Request Mechanisms**:
- [ ] Two or more methods to submit know/delete/correct requests
- [ ] Toll-free number (for businesses with online presence)
- [ ] Web form or email address
- [ ] Process for verification
- [ ] Process for authorized agents

**Internal Processes**:
- [ ] Procedures to respond to requests within 45 days
- [ ] Verification procedures
- [ ] Record-keeping system for requests
- [ ] Training for customer-facing staff
- [ ] Data inventory and mapping
- [ ] Vendor contracts reviewed/updated

**Opt-Out/Limit**:
- [ ] Opt-out mechanism that doesn't require account
- [ ] GPC signal respected
- [ ] Process to notify third parties
- [ ] Limit mechanism for sensitive PI (if applicable)

**Monitoring**:
- [ ] Annual privacy policy review and update
- [ ] Track and report request metrics
- [ ] Regular compliance assessments
- [ ] Monitor for regulatory updates
